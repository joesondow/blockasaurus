Frequently Asked Questions

How many usernames can I block at once?

Anecdotally, it seems like blocking more than about 20 or 30 accounts at a time tends to result in a lot of skipped ones. I'm looking into ways to fix that, but it's tricky so it may be a while.

Can I paste in a list of screen names with stuff like "@/" and "@." in front of them?

Yes. Blockasaurus ignores all special characters. It looks for valid twitter handles and skips everything else. Newlines, spaces, commas, @-symbols, dots, slashes, whatever. A valid Twitter handle is any contiguous string of 4 to 15 characters that consist solely of underscores, letters, and /or numbers.

Can I block accounts by numerical user ID instead of @-screenname?

Not at this time, no.

Why does Blockasaurus ask you to authenticate your Twitter account again every time you use it?

Because it doesn't store your credentials so it doesn't remember you. This also means there is no storage for bad actors to hack into.

Why does Blockasaurus ask for so many permissions to do stuff in my Twitter account?

Twitter doesn't have fine-grained access controls. They should but they don't. The permissions list you see on the authentication screen is what Twitter calls "read/write" access, which is the only way Twitter allows an app to block accounts for you.

Why should I trust Blockasaurus not to do something bad with my account credentials?

Trust is earned

You should be skeptical of every application that you use for interacting with your Twitter account, and use your best judgement about which app creators you trust. Blockasaurus is maintained by me, @JoeSondow, and it's your decision whether to trust me. If you know me you might find me trustworthy. If not, read on for options.

Revoke permissions

It's a good idea to remove any lingering application permissions that you no longer need from your Twitter account. You can safely do this after each use of Blockasaurus if you like. Maybe delete a few others you don't need anymore while you're in there too.

Options that don't require trusting me include:

Code review

Read the source code, or ask a JavaScript-savvy friend to read it for you. Check that it doesn't do anything bad.

Roll your own

Remix the application by clicking the fishies in the upper right corner and clicking "Remix on Glitch". This will give you your own copy of the code to modify, and your own Glitch application URL. Register your own Twitter application with a callback URL matching your Glitch app with "/access-token" appended to the URL. Next, get your Twitter app's credentials and put them in the .env file of your Glitch app. Now you have your own version of Blockasaurus that does not interact with anything I have access to. As a bonus, you can also modify your version to suit your liking.
